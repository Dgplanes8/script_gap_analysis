The #1 Mistake: Changing the AI-Optimized Hook

They get the AI-generated concept that scored 23/25, then think "I can make this sound more like our brand" and completely rewrite it. The performance prediction drops to 8/25.

Look, I get it. Your first instinct is to "polish" the AI copy to match your brand voice.

But here's what you need to understand: The AI didn't randomly generate that hook. It identified specific psychological patterns that your target audience responds to based on analysis of 10,000+ high-performing creatives.

The Right Way to Use AI Creative Concepts:

✅ Test the concept exactly as provided first
Get baseline performance data before making changes
✅ Keep the psychological triggers intact
Maintain the core pattern that drives performance
✅ Adapt the language, not the structure
Change words to match your brand while preserving the psychology
✅ A/B test variations systematically
Use performance data to guide optimizations

Real Example:

AI-Generated (23/25 score): "Playing a prank on my client, GONE WRONG"

Brand "Improved" Version (8/25 score): "Here's an interesting case study about our client experience"

Result: The "polished" version got 67% fewer clicks.

The psychological trigger in the AI version was "pattern interrupt + curiosity." The brand version removed both triggers and made it boring.

Bottom line: Trust the AI scoring system. If it predicts 20+ performance, test it exactly as provided. You can always optimize later based on real data.
